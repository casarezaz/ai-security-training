# Scenario 5: Defending Against Deepfake Social Engineering

## Situation

An attacker used real-time deepfake video and voice cloning to impersonate your CEO on a video call with the CFO, authorizing a $4.2M wire transfer to a fraudulent account. The attack succeeded because the deepfake was convincing enough to pass visual and auditory inspection by a senior executive who knows the CEO well.

## What Makes This Hard

Deepfake quality has reached the point where human detection is unreliable. The attack combined AI-generated video with social engineering (the call happened during a period when the CEO was traveling and unreachable by other channels). Technical deepfake detection models exist but have high false positive rates on compressed video call feeds.

## Your Mission

Design a comprehensive defense strategy combining AI-based deepfake detection, process controls (multi-factor authorization for financial transactions), and employee training. Evaluate available deepfake detection technologies and their limitations in real-time video call scenarios. Design a detection pipeline that could be integrated with your video conferencing platform.

## Success Criteria

A multi-layered defense proposal that doesn't rely solely on AI detection (which can be evaded) but combines technical controls, process controls, and human factors into a resilient system.
