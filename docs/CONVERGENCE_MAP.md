# Why AI × Cybersecurity

These two fields aren't just complementary — they're becoming inseparable. The threat landscape has evolved beyond what humans can monitor manually, and AI systems themselves have become high-value targets. This guide is designed to build skills at their intersection, where the highest demand and biggest impact exist.

## The Convergence Map

There are three fundamental ways these disciplines interlock, and every project in this guide targets at least one:

| AI FOR Security | Security OF AI | Shared Foundations |
|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Using ML to detect threats, analyze malware, predict attacks, automate response, and process threat intelligence at scale | Protecting ML models from adversarial attacks, data poisoning, model theft, prompt injection, and supply chain compromise | Data analysis, pattern recognition, anomaly detection, network traffic analysis, statistical reasoning, and adversarial thinking |

## Market Reality

The cybersecurity industry faces a global workforce shortage of **3.4 million professionals**. Within that gap, AI/ML security specialists command premium salaries because they sit at the most critical intersection: organizations need people who can both build intelligent defense systems AND secure the AI systems they deploy. This guide positions you to fill that exact gap.
